About Me

A Life Rooted in Curiosity

My fascination with the natural world began long before I understood the word “zoology.” It started with watching birds nest outside my window, following trails of ants across a garden path, and wondering why certain creatures only appeared after the rain. That childhood wonder never really left — it simply grew into a discipline.

Today, as a Zoology student, I spend my time studying animal behavior, ecological relationships, and the intricate systems that hold nature together. I believe that understanding wildlife is the first step toward protecting it, and every species, however small, has a story worth listening to.

Outside the classroom, I find peace in quiet forests, riverbanks, and early mornings spent watching the world wake up. Nature isn't just my field of study — it's where I feel most at home.

Looking Ahead

Future Goals

My ambition is to pursue advanced research in zoology, focusing on the intersection of biodiversity and conservation science. I hope to contribute to a deeper understanding of how ecosystems respond to environmental change, and to take part in field studies that give a voice to species that cannot speak for themselves. Beyond research, I want to raise environmental awareness in my community — helping others see that conservation isn't a distant cause, but a daily responsibility. In the years ahead, I hope to build an academic career that bridges scientific rigor with genuine care for the living world, so that the wild places I love today remain wild for the generations that follow.
